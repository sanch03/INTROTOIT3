Overview

Topic

We intend to develop a webpage and a phone application that will allow users to print QR codes or write NFC tags to aid in a treasure hunt, it concurrently also be called a scavenger hunt. The host can configure the game in a variety of settings, such as allocating players to teams o enabling beacons, which will be able to construct a specific number of them, which the computer will then translate into QR codes or similarly allow the host to put onto NFC tags, to be obtained in a specific order. The champion will be then contacted, and the presenter will be able to witness all of the progression on their tablet.

If this project is successful, primary/secondary schools and event organisers will be able to quickly set up a treasure hunt game. It will save them a lot of time because they only need to do a little physical labour to put it up, and for public games, the general public will get to experience intriguing QR codes that guide them around areas, however scanning a random QR code is worrying, as this could lead into an induvial such as a hacker getting access to the person’s phone who scanned the QR code, however we are trying our best to help our participants to only scan the correct QR codes. If government put up a game that encompasses a city, it can result in significant participation within the area, particularly among visitors.

Motivation

As a group and as a collective, our motivation behind this project is to get primary and secondary school children and also adults to become more physically active, as this project will help primary and secondary school children and adults to go out of their comfort zone and the comfort of their home and become more competitive, hence making anyone who participates more physically active. This project, from our group’s perspective, is interesting and important because of a few reasons, which are as follow: a QR code treasure hunt is essentially an exerciser that you can utilise inspire children or adults to be active while locating QR codes concealed across for example school, classroom, or local neighbourhood and secondly, scavenger hunts are popular amongst youngsters in schools, business offices, celebrations, and promotional events. It is also utilised as an orientation exerciser, team-building event, and professional engagement tool, in addition to being a fun game. When talking about how our fun and enjoyable game fits within the current IT trends, it can be said that whenever an individual or a visitor enters a building or a premises, they have to scan a QR code to ‘check in’, signifying the relation of it to the current IT.

Landscape

There are countless similar systems or products which are out there in the world which are available to the world, which are very closely aligned with our game, but saying that, our game is/will be extremely unique when compared to other similar games. Out in the real world, there are several competitors, which a bonus to every business, as that allows every company to outwork the other company, hence making technology more advanced. Our competitors include: Beaconstac’s QR Code solution, QR-Code-generator, QR Code Monkey, Scanova, the-qrcode-generator, GoQR, Shopify’s QR code generator and QR Stuff. When it comes to talking about points of differences there are about our project when compared to what exist now, it can be said that the project is very similar to what already exists in world, as there are already existing games which are based of scanning QR Codes and revolved around it being a scavenger hunt. The stuff that’s out in the world, the main difference between our project and the projects which already exists is the way they operate their game, as they way other competitors run their game.
