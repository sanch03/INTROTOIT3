interview feedback

In order to help us to have a better understanding about the IT industry, we invite an IT professional named Eric Zhang who works at Coles to tell us about his work experience. Interviewing an IT professional will expose our biases and ignorance. More than anything else, interviewing them will show us about what IT works looks like.

What has been done by the IT professional?

Eric tells us that he is a Systems Engineer at End User Computing 3rd level support. His main role in his work is to look after IT projects from end users’ perspective end to end. And also tech support. Other than that, Eric will look after all the audiovisual requirements in Coles because his main areas of focus are IPTel and AV. He supports all collaboration and audio-visual requirements within the company and also does general desktop support for c-suit executives. The aspects of his work that he spends most of time on is project management, that will be project planning, budgets within the project, timelines and also resources for the project, such as equipment, people and all other stuff relative to the project. And his deliverable is generally system design and implementation tasks.

The people they interact with

Interacting with other people is often required in Eric's work. Typically they cover all areas of the business. Starting from the business sponsor who’s funding the project, the external vendors who participate in the project and provide the hardware support and help with the design. At the sametime, he obviously needs to work with different skill groups to form a design and come up with the solution. Also, he will be interacting with all aspects of IT because they have multiple touch points in their projects. But typically the teams he works with day in day out is the network department. Network to IT is like the water to fish because everything runs on it. For Coles, they hold their own EFT, payment exchange and stuff like that. So security is also the department he is always interacting with. Other than that will be the database analysis pool.

The main work

After analysing the responses of the interviewee, I discovered that IT workers spend the majority of their time doing work about work, such as contacting someone about plans or doing practical, non-creative chores like micro-managing others.I also came to the conclusion that most IT personnel spend relatively little time doing skill-based tasks. IT professionals spend a portion of their time in meetings with co-workers. I also came to the conclusion that IT professionals love their jobs, participate in enjoyable activities with their co-workers, enjoy their work time, and prepare coffee in their spare time. IT employees waste a significant amount of time at work on tasks that are unrelated to their job function, greatly limiting their ability to do meaningful work. IT specialists contribute significantly to the firm; they prepare and make presentations, and they may even come up with new business ideas. IT workers do duties such as programming (Python, JavaScript, C++, and so on), data input, data management, software development, web development, software experimentation, software research, and so on. An IT professional's life is both excellent and awful. It's nice in the sense that he works in his office on his computer and doesn't have to perform any physical labour. And it's awful in the sense that if an IT worker has a lot of work to do and finishes it before the deadline, he'll have to take it home and finish it there, which will make his life more stressful and leave him with less time to spend with his family and no time to participate in other activities such as sports and going on outings.

Challenging

IT professional workers have many responsibilities in their job. From my previous interview with an IT worker, he is clearly seen as having several responsibilities such as Project planning, budgets, timelines and resources, system design, and implementation, which could be difficult to manage sometimes. From my interview responses, I understand that IT professionals love their job and enjoy what they do. Even though completing a project increases their satisfaction level, there are numerous challenges they face. After analysing the responses of the interviewee, I observe that IT workers are challenged with some things such as working with other business units and other parts. Since they are busy working on their position, sometimes it is difficult working on some parts of IT such as security, network, etc. Moreover, most of the time the IT professionals spent their time working on projects. Their excitement is after they have done their project so they really try to finish it early as quickly as possible, however, In a fast-moving project, getting timely responses and decisions from other teams is really challenging for them.

The connectivity to IT is more the same as water. IT employees spend the significant amounts of time undertaking work which are related, or performing mundane, quasi tasks. In summery many IT professionals do take minimal time performing duties related to their carriers.
